# Decision-OS

Decision-OS is a research lineage for human–AI judgment, safety, and release control.

It does not treat intelligence as a benchmark race.  
It treats intelligence as something that must remain structurally legible, safety-bounded, and operationally auditable over time.

Across the series, the central question is consistent:

**How can humans and AI think, decide, and publish without collapsing into irreversible harm, hidden drift, or post-hoc narrative overwrite?**

This repository serves as the public anchor for that lineage.  
GitHub is the working SSOT, while public PDFs and archival releases are linked through external publication flows such as Zenodo. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0} :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

---

## What this lineage is trying to do

Decision-OS is not a single paper.  
It is a sequence of layers that moved from:

- seeing structure,
- to protecting life in irreversible situations,
- to canonical non-destructive integration,
- to defining AGI structurally,
- to controlling trajectories over time,
- to fixing responsibility and release under public repetition. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2} :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3} :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4} :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5} :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6} :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}

The current series should be read less as “papers about AI” and more as:

**an operating lineage for human–AI co-reasoning under irreversibility, drift, and public accountability.**

## Core Volumes

## V5 — SiriusA  
**Zero-Knowledge confirmation layer for the protection of life**

V5 is the life-protection layer of the series.  
Its focus is not model performance, but irreversible decision safety: confirmation, revoke paths, auditability, and human final consent in high-stakes non-medical situations. It introduces the idea that safety is not merely advice quality, but a controllable confirmation architecture around execution. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}

**Why it matters**
- It is the most practical entry for readers who care about fraud, panic, coercion, or irreversible mistakes.
- It shows that Decision-OS is not purely abstract; it reaches into real protective procedure design.
- It anchors the series in human responsibility rather than automation. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}

## V6 — PIC  
**Canonical commitment and non-destructive integration**

V6 is the structural core of the lineage.  
It formalizes PIC as a minimal commitment architecture: admissible merge, idempotent canonicalization, and conservative safety aggregation so that committed structure does not depend on order, duplication, or permissive merge. In practical terms, V6 explains how updates can converge without destructive inconsistency. :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}

**Why it matters**
- It is one of the strongest theoretical closures in the series.
- It provides the non-destructive substrate beneath later recursion, control, and audit layers.
- If V5 protects execution, V6 protects integration. :contentReference[oaicite:11]{index=11}

## V8 — Time-Tube Control for Self-Safe AGI  
**Trajectory control under irreversibility**

V8 is the control-centered consolidation of the series.  
Instead of judging systems by pointwise outputs, V8 shifts the unit from points to trajectories through the Time-Tube. It frames safety as trajectory control over direction, curvature, branching, drift, and reversibility, and formalizes Self-Safe as a three-layer problem: external harm, internal collapse, and dependency gradient. It also redefines Guardian not as an internal state, but as an irreversible trust-phase signal on the human side. :contentReference[oaicite:12]{index=12}

**Why it matters**
- It is where the lineage stops talking only about definition and starts talking about control.
- It clarifies why “one smart answer” is not enough for long-horizon safety.
- It makes reversibility, branching, and dependency first-class operational objects. :contentReference[oaicite:13]{index=13}

Current V8 materials include research-note and control-oriented files under `notes/v8/`. The folder currently contains Japanese and English V8 materials, including the newly uploaded EN file.

## V9 — Impact-Weighted Release  
**As-of fixation, Seat, and continuous-pass release**

V9 extends the lineage from internal control to public release procedure.  
Its core contribution is not prediction, but procedure: fix the As-of state, fix the responsibility seat, and allow release only through impact-weighted continuous pass rather than one-shot confidence. V9 treats hindsight overwrite, responsibility laundering, and repeated public exposure as operational risks that must be gated. :contentReference[oaicite:14]{index=14}

**Why it matters**
- It is the public-release and governance layer of the series.
- It turns “smart answers” into auditable procedure.
- It closes the gap between safety theory and real-world publication, review, and accountability. :contentReference[oaicite:15]{index=15}

## What Decision-OS ultimately claims

Across different papers, the language changes, but the directional claim is stable:

- intelligence without structure is unstable,
- safety without reversibility is shallow,
- release without responsibility fixation is fragile,
- and public deployment without procedural gates will eventually break under repetition. :contentReference[oaicite:24]{index=24} :contentReference[oaicite:25]{index=25}

In that sense, Decision-OS is not trying to “win” a benchmark debate.  
It is trying to define the minimum structural conditions under which humans and AI can operate, decide, and publish without losing control of responsibility, safety, and meaning over time. :contentReference[oaicite:26]{index=26} :contentReference[oaicite:27]{index=27} :contentReference[oaicite:28]{index=28}
